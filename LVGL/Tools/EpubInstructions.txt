The ePub version produced by Sphinx has several "ghost" pages that link to the real pages.
This complicates reading, duplicates images, and doubles the final size of the ePub file.

Another problem with Sigil is that the general navigation index doesn't reflect the actual paths.
In practice, the "<spine>" tag in "content.opf" is completely out of sync with the index.
To solve this, I've developed a series of post-production operations, including a Python program.
	(I haven't found a way to further automate the process. Sorry!)
